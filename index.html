Pakistan Tax System

Understanding Tax Law in Pakistan

Pakistan's tax system is administered federally by the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR). Every person with taxable income must file an annual return — whether individual, AOP, or company.

📊
Income Tax Rates 2024–25
Individuals are taxed progressively from 5% to 35% based on total income. Companies are assessed at 29% (banking companies at 35%). Super tax applies to high-income entities.
Individual: 5%–35% Corporate: 29% Banking: 35% Super Tax: applicable
🏢
Income Classification Heads
All income in Pakistan is classified under specific heads for tax computation purposes — each with its own rules, exemptions, and deductions under the Income Tax Ordinance 2001.
Salaries Business / Profession Property Capital Gains Other Sources
🌐
Active vs Non-Active Filer
Pakistan maintains an Active Taxpayer List (ATL). Active filers enjoy lower withholding tax rates on property, vehicles, banking transactions, and dividends — making timely filing financially beneficial.
